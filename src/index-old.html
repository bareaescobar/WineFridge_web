Ideal Temperature for Wine Storage:

Red Wine

55°F (13°C) is considered the perfect temperature for aging red wines. Slightly warmer temperatures, around 57-59°F (14-15°C), can be acceptable but avoid anything above 65°F (18°C) as it accelerates aging and may cause the wine to age prematurely.

White Wine

50-55°F (10-13°C) is the ideal range for storing white wines. White wines are generally best stored cooler than reds, but not too cold, which could dull their flavors.

Sparkling Wines & Champagne

45°F (7°C) is the best for sparkling wines, as it helps preserve their effervescence and delicate aromas.

Port & Dessert Wines

These wines tend to be stored at around 60°F (15°C), which is slightly warmer than regular reds but still within a manageable range for aging.
